What is MannChill? A mental health nudging app that decomposes allostatic load into financial, physiological, and behavioral components — calibrated for the unique stressors of the Nepali diaspora. It outputs a continuous stress score (0-100) plus culturally-adapted, bilingual (English + Nepali) actionable nudges. Not a clinical diagnostic tool.

The core insight: For the Nepali diaspora, financial stress IS the health problem. Remittance burden — the obligation to send money home — is the single strongest predictor of poor mental health in this population (Simkhada et al., 2021). Yet no existing wellness app models remittance burden as a health signal.

Generic wellness apps ask "how are you feeling?" MannChill asks "what percentage of your income goes to remittances?" — because for 6+ million Nepali diaspora workers, the answer to that question predicts their mental health better than any mood slider.

Key research finding: "Family problems compounded by constant financial burdens and unmet expectations were the most important factors linked with poor mental health" among Nepali migrant workers. — Simkhada et al., 2021 (J Migr Health)

2. Seven Key Features

Feature 1: Culturally-Specific Stress Model

We don't just ask "how are you feeling." We decompose stress into 4 weighted dimensions calibrated for the Nepali diaspora:

DimensionWeightRationale
Financial40%Remittance burden, debt, income instability — dominant stressor per Simkhada et al. (2021)
HRV + Sleep25%Heart rate variability + sleep quality — physiological stress biomarkers
Behavioral20%Screen time, steps, exercise — modifiable lifestyle factors
Self-Report15%Mood + stress ratings — subjective validation of objective signals

The 40% financial weight exists because Simkhada et al. (2021) found financial burden is the single strongest predictor of poor mental health in migrant populations. No other app does this.

Feature 2: Real Wearable Data — Live WHOOP Integration

We built a full OAuth2 flow to pull real biometric data from WHOOP's API:

  • HRV (RMSSD) in milliseconds
  • Sleep duration and sleep efficiency
  • Resting heart rate
  • Recovery score and strain score
  • Weekly exercise minutes (7-day aggregate)

This isn't synthetic — it's your actual biometrics feeding directly into the scoring engine. Connect your device, and your stress score updates with real physiological data.

User clicks Connect → WHOOP OAuth login → Token exchange → Pull cycles, recovery, sleep, workouts → Feed into scoring engine

Feature 4: AI-Powered Voice Journaling with Acoustic Stress Analysis

Users record a voice journal entry. Google Gemini 2.5 Flash analyzes both the transcript content AND acoustic features:

Transcript sentimentStress/calm keywords, emotional contentLLM analysis
Pitch varianceStressed speech = higher, more variable pitchAcoustic

Blending: 70% LLM analysis + 30% acoustic features. Falls back to acoustic-only if Gemini is unavailable. The voice stress score (0-10) is blended into the self-report sub-score: 60% slider + 40% voice.

Feature 5: Remittance Burden as a First-Class Health Signal

77% of Nepali households depend on remittances. 27% of Nepal's GDP is remittances. We model three financial health inputs:

MetricInternal WeightStress Escalation
Remittance-to-income ratio50%<10% low → 10-20% moderate → 20-30% elevated → >30% severe
Debt-to-monthly-income30%<2x low → 2-4x moderate → 4-6x elevated → >6x severe
Income stability (0-1)20%Direct inverse: 0 = max stress, 1 = no stress

The app generates culturally-adapted nudges that respect family obligation (izzat/इज्जत) while suggesting sustainable limits. Example: "Your remittance burden is above the sustainable threshold. Consider a family conversation about a temporary reduction — protecting your health protects your ability to support them long-term."

Feature 6: Actionable, Not Just Informational

The app doesn't just show a number. It gives you:

  • What-if scenario simulator — "What happens to my score if I reduce remittance by 5%?" with real-time projected score, delta badge, and dominant stressor shift
  • Weekly routine builder — 15 checkable items across Sleep, Movement, Financial, Mind, and Screen Time categories with progress tracking
  • Tiered resources by severity band — Low/Moderate/High/Severe, each with curated community, financial, mental health, and crisis resources
  • Bilingual nudges — English + Nepali, culturally adapted, 1-4 per assessment prioritized by dominant stressor

3. Scoring Model Deep Dive

Composite Score Formula

Allostatic Load = (Financial × 0.40) + (HRV+Sleep × 0.25) + (Behavioral × 0.20) + (Self-Report × 0.15)

Output: 0-100 continuous score. All sub-scores are independently 0-100.

Severity Bands & K6 Equivalence

Score RangeBandK6 EquivalentColor
0 – 25LowNo significant distressGreen
26 – 50ModerateMild-moderate distressYellow
51 – 75HighModerate-severe distressOrange
76 – 100SevereSevere distressRed

Note: MannChill uses a custom 0-100 allostatic load index. The K6 (Kessler et al., 2003) uses a 0-24 range. We map severity language, not scores.

  • Voice stress (optional, 0-10): If present, blended 60% slider + 40% voice

7. AI-Powered Voice Journaling

Record audio → Web Speech API transcribes → Extract pitch, RMS, speech rate → Send to Gemini 2.5 Flash → Blend 70% LLM + 30% acoustic → Voice stress score (0-10)

Acoustic Feature Extraction

FeatureMethodStress Indicator
Pitch (Hz)Autocorrelation algorithm (60-500 Hz)Higher pitch = more stress
Energy (RMS)Web Audio API analyser nodeHigher energy = more stress
Speech RateWords per minute calculationFaster speech = more stress
SentimentStress/calm keyword matching + Gemini LLMNegative sentiment = more stress

Gemini LLM Prompt Context

The prompt instructs Gemini to analyze voice characteristics (tone, pace, hesitations, vocal energy) and content across stress domains: finances, remittances, work, sleep, and general wellbeing. It returns a structured JSON with stress_score (0-10), sentiment label, reasoning text, and keywords.

Score Integration

Final self-report sub-score = 60% slider input + 40% voice stress. The voice component itself = 70% Gemini LLM + 30% acoustic features. If Gemini is unavailable, falls back to acoustic-only with keyword sentiment.

9. Cultural Adaptations

Design Principles

PrincipleImplementation
Izzat (इज्जत) — Honor/Respect Nudges acknowledge family obligation as a core value. Never suggest cutting off remittances — instead frame health protection as enabling long-term family support. Example: "Protecting your health protects your ability to support them long-term."
Remittance as Health Signal Modeled as 50% of financial sub-score (which is 40% of total). Includes remittance renegotiation guide in resources. Cultural note on Financial Detail page: "These figures are for awareness, not judgement."
Bilingual Support All critical nudges in English + Nepali. Crisis resources include Nepali-language phone lines. App title in Nepali: मनशान्ति. Resource cards with Nepali translations where available.
Community-First Model Resource escalation: Nepali community centers (low) → South Asian peer support/SNEHA (moderate) → Nepali helplines/NAFCON (high) → Crisis lines with Nepali interpreters (severe). Western clinical systems are supplementary, not primary.
Non-Pathologizing Language "Nudges" not "treatment." Stress framed as a normal response to real stressors. No "you are at risk" language. Supportive tone throughout.
